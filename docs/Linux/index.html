    • Linux

    If I remember correctly, I got introduced to Linux in 2003. I vaguely remember installing and crashing the home computer. I don't think anyone else bothered too much about it; I was constantly trying out something with the home computer. With little guidance and no internet I ditched the effort and went back to Windows. Much later in 2006 I picked it up again, this time influenced by How To Become A Hacker 1. How To Become A Hacker by Eric Raymond . Something about the openness and the hackability lured me over.

    Years have passed on and now I have moved out of developing code for a living. Although in the software industry, I now manage people. This took me away from Linux for my day to day work. But my personal laptop still runs Linux.
